Staff

Meet the Pioneer Indoor 2017 staff and design team

Adam Hill

Director

Adam Hill is an educator/percussionist in the Chicagoland area. He is a Music Education Graduate with a percussion emphasis from VanderCook College of Music under the instruction of Kevin Lepper and Dr. Charlie Menghini. He currently is the Executive Director and founder of the 2 time world champion Pioneer Indoor an Independent WGI marching percussion ensemble. Hill is also director of percussion, music technology, music theory, guitar and assistant marching band director at Romeoville High School in Romeoville IL. Hill is also employed by the Chicago Bears as a member of the Chicago Bears drumline and with the Chicago Bulls as a member of the “Stampede“ Drumline. He was also a member of and percussion coordinator/ caption head and arranger for the Pioneer Drum and Bugle Corps. Hill has Placed with in the top ten for snare drum solo contest in Drum Corp International, Drum Corps Midwest, PASIC, and in 2002 he placed 2nd at DCI snare drum solo competition. Hill is also a recipient of the Dr. Rosenthal Excellent teachers award from Vandercook College of Music. Adam Hill is a very active music arranger, judge, and clinician around the Chicagoland. He is also very proud to be a Pro-Mark, Evans and Mapex/Majestic, Zildjian Percussion endorser.

Don Click

Program Coordinator, Visual Design

Don Click is co-founder of the Music City Mystique youth organization and served as the Executive Director until 2006. He currently serves on the Board of Directors and is a member of the design team. Don attended the University of North Texas where he earned a degree in Finance. While at UNT, Don spent three years as a member of the UNT indoor drumline. During this time, the drumline won two championships at the Percussive Arts Society International Convention. Don also has deep roots in the Drum and Bugle Corps (DCI) activity. Don was a member of the 1985 Bridgemen, the 1988 Suncoast Sound, and the 1989 Concord Blue Devils. He has taught and continues to arrange and consult at various high schools throughout the nation and is a sought after judge both for fall marching band competitions as well as Winter Percussion shows. Don has served as President of the Southeastern Color Guard Circuit where he was formerly the Percussion Coordinator. With Winter Guard International, Don has held the responsibilities of being a member of the Percussion Advisory Board, Percussion Steering Committee, and is a current member of the Board of Directors and Executive Committee serving as Secretary. He was inducted into the Winter Guard International Hall of Fame in 2010. Don currently lives in Katy, TX with his wife, Stephani, and two children, Avery and Piper.

Bryan Harmsen

Music Arranger, Show Design

Bryan is the owner of Visaudio Designs. He has arranged for and been a clinician and instructor for a number of schools around the country, primarily in Texas. He is a music arranger with a focus in percussion arranging and his arrangements and compositions have helped many ensembles earn numerous gold medals on their local circuits as well as finalist positions and the 2011 PSO SIlver medalist at WGI World Championships. Bryan was a member of a number of drum and bugle corps including Spirit of Atlanta, 2000, Southwind, 2001 and 2002, and The Cavaliers for 2003 and the 2004 championship season. Recently, Bryan has also started giving clinics and master classes on using the Virtual Drumline sound library from Tapspace Publications. Contact him if you would be interested in setting one up in your area. While not working on music, Bryan also dabbles in web and graphic design and has an affinity for all things on the leading edge of technology. He currently resides in Louisville, Kentucky with his wife Emily and their two dogs and two cats, Roxy, Wilson, Ralphie, and Bella.

Andy Dittrich

Battery Caption Head

Andy Dittrich is a percussion instructor and clinician in the Chicagoland area. A graduate of the University of Illinois and a nine-year drum corps veteran, Andy has become a sought-after and respected marching percussion teacher and consultant. His long drum corps career included time with Capital Sound (Madison, WI), Southwind (Lexington, KY), under T.R and Patrick Fitz-Gibbon, and the Madison Scouts, under Roger Carter. Andy also spent four years performing with the Illini Drumline of the University of Illinois. Since aging out, he has performed professionally with the drumlines for both the Chicago Bears and Chicago Bulls.

Andy has served as an instructor or consultant for many of Illinois’ top high school programs and since 2009, has been the snare tech for Pioneer Indoor. he was named Battery Caption Head for Pioneer Indoor for the 2014 season, and will continue in this role for a 2nd year in 2015.

Tom Moffitt

Front Ensemble Caption Head/Sound Engineer

Tom Moffitt is currently a student at Columbia College Chicago, pursuing a degree in Audio Arts and Acoustics, and is a member of the Chicago Bulls Stampede Drumline. With over a decade of experience in the fields of percussion, guitar and piano, Tom is primarily a recording musician and music educator in the Chicagoland area. Throughout his music career, Tom has received education from Ben Wahlund, Bret Kuhn, Dr. Greg Beyer, Robert Chappell, Roger Carter, Nick Angelis, Jeff Lee, Vince Oliver, Dr. Brad Meyer, and Dr. Andrew Bliss.

Tom began his experience in the realm of Front Ensemble throughout high school, and continued his endeavors as a member of the 2007 Pioneer Indoor Front Ensemble, as well as the Madison Scouts Front Ensemble through the 2007 to 2011 seasons, serving as section leader in both organizations. Tom was also a member of the Northern Illinois University Huskie Marching Band for four years, serving as an Undergraduate Assistant.

Given his tenure as a recording musician, Tom is experienced in professional audio configurations and live sound reinforcement settings. Tom also serves as the Sound Engineer for the Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps, a position he has held since 2013. During the Fall season, Tom works with Naperville Central High School as a Marching Percussion Instructor. This will be Tom’s sixth season serving Pioneer Indoor as a front ensemble instructor.

Rob Elston

Front Ensemble Tech

Rob Elston is an educator and freelance percussionist based in the St. Louis metro area. Currently he is the front ensemble caption head at Belleville East High School, battery caption head at Parkway South High School, and for the 2016 season he will join WGI finalist Edwardsville High School as the front ensemble caption head. In addition, Elston also maintains a private lesson studio and is a sought after clinician. Rob has presented masterclasses/clinics at institutions such as Cal Arts, McKendree University, Tennessee Tech University, The Bob Cole Conservatory of Music, Machine Project, and Chapman University. He has adjudicated for the Midwest Percussion Co-op, Illinois Music Educators Association, and the Percussive Arts Society. Elston also co-chairs the high school portion of the PAS Percussion Ensemble Committee.

As a performer, Rob is a multi-percussionist who fuses his eclectic knowledge of percussion, and innate musicality to create a unique and commanding musical voice. His marching arts experience includes time as a member of the snare line for Capital Sound, and Southwind’s front ensemble. After aging out in 2004 Elston returned to teach the front ensemble at Southwind for three seasons. More recently he was a full time freelancing musician in Los Angeles from 2005-2010. During this time he frequently performed with groups such as MESTO (Multi-Ethnic Star Orchestra), The Bossa Nova Group, Bloco Chico, Lian Ensemble, 18 Squared, Kamran and Hooman, The Procrastinators, and The LA All Star Drumline. Rob also has recording credits for movies such as Seal Team VI, and artists such as Clinton Patterson, Bruno Louchouarn, MESTO, Terri Card Heller, Zaub Nasty, Scott Collins, and Gamelan Sekar Jaya. He has performed throughout the United States, and has studied/performed internationally in Brazil and the United Arab Emirates.

Elston holds a Bachelor of Music degree from Eastern Illinois University, and a Master of Fine Arts degree in multi-focus percussion from the California Institute of the Arts. He has studied closely with John Bergamo, Swapan Chaudhuri, Randy Gloss, Andrew Grueschow, David Johnson, Alfred Ladezekpo, Johnny Lee Lane, Houman Pourmedhi, Aaron Serfaty, and Eric Willie.

Rebecca Falls

Front Ensemble Tech

Rebecca Falls is a music educator in the Chicagoland area. She received a Bachelor’s Degree in Music Education from VanderCook College of Music under the direction of Kevin Lepper and Dr. Charlie Menghini. Rebecca is currently a band director at Arbor Park Middle School in Oak Forest, IL.

Rebecca began her musical career in the front ensemble through her high school marching band experience, and continued her endeavors by being a member of the Pioneer Indoor Front Ensemble for six years. She was also a member of the Front Ensemble for the Pioneer Drum and Bugle Corps in 2009 as well as the Colts in 2012.

During the fall season, Rebecca works with the front ensembles at both Romeoville High School and Minooka High School. This will be Rebecca’s second season serving Pioneer Indoor as a front ensemble instructor.

Amanda Petersen

Andy Hull

Battery Tech

Andy Hull performed with two different drum corps' over 5 seasons. He started at 16 years old with Capital Sound Drum and Bugle Corps on bass 1 in 2003. He later joined Blue Stars Drum and Bugle Corps in 2005 on snare and continued until 2008 where the corps took 8th place in DCI finals for the first time in 32 years. In high school and drum corps, Andy has marched under the instruction of two previous Pioneer Indoor staff members, Allan Zwettler and Brandon Estes. During Blue Stars 2006-2008 he marched under the instruction of Brent Montgomery who was the battery head for the 1st place percussion section of Star of Indiana 1993.

Andy is a 2009 Bradley University graduate with a degree in Computer Information Systems. The summer before he graduated he started an internship with Country Financial in Bloomington, IL where he was later offered a full time position. After a few years of work experience, Andy decided to return to his Chicagoland roots and found a job at Vivid Seats, a competitor to Stub Hub, in January 2013 where he currently works as a software engineer. Andy is also the main architect, designer, and coder of Pioneer Indoor's new, and current website.

This will be Andy's third season working with Pioneer Indoor. He was brought on as bass drum instructor for the 2013-2014 season and transitioned to snare drum instructor with Andy Dittrich for the 2014-2015 season.

Kyle Jennings

Battery Tech

Kyle Jennings has been involved with the marching arts for over 10 years as a performer, educator, consultant, and writer. As a performer, Kyle was a member of quad lines for Capital Sound Drum and Bugle Corps and Pioneer Drum and Bugle Corps and was a member of snare drum lines for Pioneer Drum and Bugle Corps, Glassmen Drum and Bugle Corps, and Matrix Indoor Percussion. Kyle has been fortunate to study with many outstanding percussion educators, including Rob Ferguson, Tyler Orbison, Stuart Spoon, Blair Williams, and Adam Hill.

As an educator, Kyle has taught, written, and consulted for various high school marching percussion programs in Indiana and Illinois in the past 7 years and was a snare drum technician for Matrix Indoor Percussion in 2013 (WGI Independent World 4th Place). After a hiatus from percussion to complete a master’s degree in clinical psychology, Kyle returned to indoor percussion education as the snare drum technician for Minooka (IL) HS Indoor Percussion (2016 WGI Scholastic A 9th Place).

A native of Pekin, IL, he now resides in Bolingbrook, IL with his wife, Kirsten, and their greyhound, Breesy, and practices as a psychotherapist in Aurora, IL.

Bobby Bonslater

Cymbal Tech

Bobby Bonslater began his marching arts career in 2001 playing baritone for Racine Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps. He advanced to become the Trumpet Soloist for the 2004 DCI Midwest Div III Champions, Americanos Drum and Bugle Corps, the 2005 DCI Division II/III Grand Champion, East Coast Jazz Drum and Bugle Corps and the Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps, where he aged out in 2007. Bobby continued to march at the all-age competitive level with the 3-time DCA World Champions, Govenaires Drum and Bugle Corps, while serving as Program Coordinator for the Racine Scouts who performed twice as DCI Open Class Semi-finalist under his leadership. In addition to his extensive Drum Corps background, Bobby marched Cymbals for Americanos and Pioneer Indoor Percussion Ensembles earning two WGI World Championships. This will be Bobby's first year back with Pioneer Indoor. Bobby is currently working with Maine West High School of Des Plaines, IL as Percussion Instructor and Show Designer, Shadow Armada of Oregon, WI as Visual Coordinator/Woodwind Tech and the Govenaires of St. Peter, MN as Brass Caption head.

Eric Hart

Cymbal Tech

Eric Hart is a professional instructor, studio musician, and live percussionist from Chicago, IL.

Eric is an Americanos Indoor and Pioneer Indoor Percussion ensemble alumni in the cymbal section; and has been involved with our organization for 6 seasons. 2016 will be his third season back with Pioneer working alongside Bobby Bonslater as a cymbal technician.

In between his time as a marching member and instructional staff member Eric studied music business and marketing at Columbia College Chicago. He has been on over 20 recordings in a variety of genres as a drum set player, percussionist, vocalist, or producer. He is a sought after live musician as well; having spent time touring and performing all over the United States.

When Eric is not involved with Pio Indoor he is a resident musician at a nightclub in Chicago performing with acclaimed DJ’s and producers from all over the world. He is an active member of Chicago’s rock music and electronic music community.

Tyler Woods

Visual Tech

Tyler Woods currently lives in Chicago, IL as a freelance instructor and musician. Aside from his work with Pioneer Indoor, Tyler is the Visual Caption Head for the Downers Grove South High School Marching Mustangs (Downers Grove, IL), a visual instructor for the Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps (Madison, WI), and a visual instructor for the Downers Grove South High School Winter Color Guard.

Having graduated from the University of Minnesota Duluth with a Bachelor’s Degree in Instrumental Music Education, Tyler’s marching experience includes three years as a member of the Madison Scouts Drum and Bugle Corps. Previous teaching experiences include working as a drill writer and instructor for the University of Minnesota Duluth Bulldog Marching Band, a visual instructor for the Crossmen Drum and Bugle Corps, and the former Head Band Director for Luverne High School (Luverne, MN).

Kelly Dittrich

Administration/Social Media

Kelly Dittrich graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Advertising from the University of Illinois, where she spent the entirety of her marching career with the Marching Illini as a member of the Illinettes Dance Team. An avid dancer, Kelly has served as a choreographer and teacher for many of Illinois’ top dance programs, and served as a camp instructor and administrator for Universal Dance Association (UDA).

Currently, Kelly is a digital marketing manager for a B2B public relations and social media marketing agency in Chicago, where she manages the online presence for a diverse client base through social media strategy, content marketing and media relations. She was appointed Pioneer Indoor’s administrative and social media manager in 2013, where she oversees financial and logistical matters for the ensemble.
